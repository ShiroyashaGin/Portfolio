Bachelor Thesis - Job interview Simulator


Abstract

The User-Centered Design department of The Hanze University of Applied Sciences had raised suspicions that new graduates from the Communication & Multimedia Design study, have issues with job interviews due to nervousness. This raised suspicion was confirmed by preliminary field research and an interview with a professional IT recruiter. The request of the UCD department was to design and develop a digital prototype that would help new graduates to feel less nervous during job interviews.

The leading suspicion towards the nervousness was due to the low level of confidence in their own skills, lack of experience with job interviews and they have no idea what level of skill the recruiters are expecting. This project aimed to solve their deficit of experience and familiarity with the job interviews. Virtual Reality Exposure Therapy was chosen as most suitable method to deal with the problem at hand. VRET proves to be at least as effective as traditional Exposure Therapy with the extra benefits of lower costs, not location bound and being able to change the components of the exposed phobia at any time. VRET is a method used to treat phobias and anxiety disorders. Nervousness is a common symptom of an anxiety disorder, thus expected to be treatable in the same manner of exposure. Exposure Therapy tries to elicit the nervousness from the user in a safe and controlled environment.

Different methods were applied with the purpose of drawing out the nervousness of the user. This however proved to be not very successful. The lack of emotional involvement and feeling of consequence left the user to be not caring enough about the situation, thus not leading to a sufficient level of nervousness being drawn out. The immersion and presence levels that are required to elicit the level of nervous however were met. A major absence that the prototype had at this stage was that the NPC was not showing an accurate enough human behavior, which led to the user feeling more as if they were interacting with a machine instead of a fellow human A more advanced AI that could more accurately simulate human behavior would be a valid improvement to the already established fundamentals present in the prototype.
